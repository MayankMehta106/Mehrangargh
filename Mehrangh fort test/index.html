Explore the Rich Heritage of Mehrangarh

Founded in 1459 by Rao Jodha, Mehrangarh Fort stands as one of India's most magnificent forts, rising 400 feet above Jodhpur's skyline.

For over five centuries, this architectural marvel has been a symbol of the Rathore dynasty's power and artistic vision, showcasing the rich cultural heritage of Rajasthan.

About Mehrangarh Fort

Mehrangarh Fort, one of the largest forts in India, stands as a testament to the rich history and architectural brilliance of Rajasthan. Perched 400 feet (122 m) above the city of Jodhpur, this imposing structure is a magnificent example of Rajput architecture.

The fort's name "Mehrangarh" derives from two words: "Mehr," meaning sun or Sun-deity (a reference to the clan's claimed descent from the Sun), and "garh," which means fort. Thus, Mehrangarh stands for "Fort of the Sun" ‚Äì a fitting name for a structure that dominates Jodhpur's skyline.

Enclosed by imposing thick walls, Mehrangarh Fort houses several palaces known for their intricate carvings and expansive courtyards. The fort complex includes numerous buildings, including the Moti Mahal (Pearl Palace), Phool Mahal (Flower Palace), Sheesh Mahal (Mirror Palace), Sileh Khana, and Daulat Khana.

Today, Mehrangarh Fort is managed by the Mehrangarh Museum Trust and serves as a museum, cultural center, and popular tourist destination. It offers visitors a glimpse into the royal heritage of Jodhpur and the splendor of Rajasthan's past.

History of Mehrangarh Fort

1459

Foundation by Rao Jodha

Mehrangarh Fort was established by Rao Jodha, the 15th ruler of the Rathore clan, when he transferred his capital from Mandore to Jodhpur. According to legend, to build the fort, Rao Jodha had to displace a hermit called Cheeria Nathji, the lord of birds. Enraged at being forced to move, the hermit cursed Jodha that the fort would suffer from scarcity of water. To counter this curse, Rao Jodha built a temple within the fort and buried a man alive in the foundations (a practice called "nara-bali" or human sacrifice) to ensure the fort's good fortune.

1543

Siege by Emperor Humayun

The fort faced its first major military challenge when Mughal Emperor Humayun briefly captured Jodhpur. This event marked the beginning of a complex relationship between the Rathores and the Mughals that would shape the fort's development over the next centuries.

1561-1652

Mota Raja Udai Singh's Expansion

Rao Udai Singh, also known as Mota Raja (the Fat King), significantly expanded the fort during his reign. He built the Moti Mahal (Pearl Palace) and strengthened the fort's defenses. During this period, the Rathores formed an alliance with the Mughals, which brought relative peace and prosperity to the region.

1678-1707

Maharaja Jaswant Singh and Aurangzeb Era

During the reign of Maharaja Jaswant Singh, relations with the Mughals deteriorated under Emperor Aurangzeb. After Jaswant Singh's death, Aurangzeb attempted to annex Marwar, leading to a 30-year war. The fort served as a crucial stronghold during this turbulent period.

1707-1752

Maharaja Ajit Singh's Reconstruction

After reclaiming Jodhpur, Maharaja Ajit Singh undertook significant reconstruction of the fort, which had suffered damage during the wars. He built the Phool Mahal (Flower Palace), one of the most opulent halls in the fort, designed for private audiences and entertainment.

1752-1843

Golden Age Under Maharaja Takhat Singh

This period saw the addition of several palaces and the fort reached its architectural zenith. Takhat Singh, who ruled from 1843-1873, added the Takhat Vilas, his personal chambers adorned with European clock faces and English calendar tiles, reflecting the growing Western influence in India.

1947-Present

Modern Era and Conservation

After India's independence in 1947, the royal family of Jodhpur converted part of the fort into a museum in 1972. The Mehrangarh Museum Trust, established by Maharaja Gaj Singh II, now manages the fort and has undertaken extensive conservation efforts to preserve this historical treasure. Today, Mehrangarh Fort stands as one of India's best-preserved forts and a premier tourist destination, attracting over a million visitors annually.

Architecture of Mehrangarh Fort

The Mehrangarh Fort spans 550 meters in width and 350 meters in length, rising 155 meters above the surrounding landscape. Its defensive walls, some as thick as 6 meters, have withstood the test of time.

The fort is accessed through seven gates, including Jai Pol, Fateh Pol, and Loha Pol, each with its own historical significance. Inside, visitors can marvel at the palaces such as Moti Mahal, Sheesh Mahal, and Phool Mahal, which showcase intricate carvings and vibrant decorations.

Hollywood Connection

Mehrangarh Fort served as a filming location for Christopher Nolan's blockbuster movie "The Dark Knight Rises" (2012). The fort was featured as the prison where Bruce Wayne (Batman) was imprisoned. The scenes of prisoners attempting to climb out of the pit were filmed here.

Music Festival

Since 2007, Mehrangarh Fort has been hosting the World Sufi Spirit Festival (also known as the World Sacred Spirit Festival), attracting musicians and performers from around the world. The festival celebrates mystical music traditions in the stunning backdrop of the fort.

Tourism Records

Mehrangarh Fort attracts over 1.5 million visitors annually, making it one of the most visited forts in Rajasthan. International tourists account for approximately 30% of all visitors, with the highest numbers coming from France, Germany, and the United Kingdom.

UNESCO Recognition

In 2013, Mehrangarh Fort received the prestigious Award of Excellence from UNESCO for cultural heritage conservation. The fort's museum was recognized for its outstanding efforts in preserving the rich cultural heritage of Rajasthan.

Flying Fox Adventure

Mehrangarh Fort offers a unique zipline experience called "Flying Fox," where visitors can zip through the air across the fort's battlements and surrounding Rao Jodha Desert Rock Park. The six ziplines provide breathtaking views of the fort and the blue city of Jodhpur below.
